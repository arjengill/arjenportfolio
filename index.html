Intro

Introduction

Welcome to my website! A little bit about me: I am a highly attentive and hard-working person that seeks to push the boundaries in whichever endeavour I am involved in. It naturally follows that I am the happiest when I am learning a new skill, or undertaking a challenging project – especially when it is with others on the same journey – because it is the instrument by which I can experience meaningful growth.

Interests

In my free time, I produce music, and write for music publications. At university (University of Nottingham), I was involved in various societies, including our Sneakers and Streetwear Society (in which I was President), Sikh Society, and Hip Hop Society. I also occasionally spend time at my local radio station, Sabras Sound, helping out with: creating instrumentals, radio advertisements, and artist interviews; updating our database of songs and movies; and organising local events.

Work

BritAsia TV

For BritAsia TV, I created comprehensive technical SEO audits covering what they need to fix with their website to improve Core Web Vitals performance, and on-page SEO audits with a content plan.

I also wrote them some in-depth pieces covering Punjabi music, from topics like 'Are less western kids listening to Punjabi music?' to music reviews of popular artists.

Punchline Punjabi

Punchline Punjabi is a passion project that seeks to create original commentaries and stories about Punjabi music. My aim with this project is to occupy a spot in at least the first three links of Google's first SERP for my targeted keywords. So far, I have managed to rank 9 articles first.

I designed the website from scratch to gain practical knowledge of how a website functions. In this project I got to develop: HTML, CSS, and JavaScript fundamentals; SEO, including how to dynamically generate Schema.org meta data with JavaScript, and Lighthouse optimisations; and writing original content for a niche target audience, through the mediums of writing, video, and music. Within the first two months I grew the site to 40,000 monthly hits, which then stabilised between 45-50,000 hits in the next two months. I also achieved good average time on page, which has been almost 4 minutes, and over 6 minutes for longer review content.

Shuperb, House of Slippers, Steel Toe Boots, & SchoolShoes

For the sibling e-commerce websites of Shuperb.co.uk, HouseofSlippers.co.uk, SteelToeBoots.co.uk, & SchoolShoes.co.uk, I used my copywriting, SEO, and front-end web development skills to achieve the best UI & UX possible.

In my role, the main questions to consider were: 'how best do I funnel our traffic towards the relevant category pages'; 'how can I improve our website performance'; 'who is our target audience, and what information do they need to make an informed purchase'; 'is implementing a new widget plugin worth the extra processing load it brings'; 'can we improve the search-engine rankings of our top pages by adding more structured markup to our HTML' – and so on...

My biggest challenge was SteelToeBoots.co.uk, which needed a big overhaul in terms of UI & UX, and so my goal was to make STB the most practical and utilitarian safety boot website it could be. The result: We managed to increase website traffic by 1,000%, and improved our bounce rates on product pages by introducing new features that made it easier for customers to get the information they needed at the right time.

BizAsia

Writing for BizAsiaLive.com was partly an exercise in transforming excessively bulky source stories into factual, and relevant articles that told our readers exactly what they wanted to know – strictly with no clickbait, and no filler. Often times, this meant collating a story from various sources, and piecing together a more cohesive narrative that never strayed away from the headline subject. I have also written exclusive stories, using our own sources to write on current events in the Brit section of the digital publication.

With Raj Baddhan's mentorship, I gained a lot of knowledge about the media industry and how informative content can be used to generate quality viewership.

The main skills I learned from writing with BizAsia were: writing extremely succinctly, proofreading at pace, learning how to find and fact-check information from non-peer-reviewed sources.

In total, I have written 166 stories so far, with a readership of over 100,000 unique readers.

National Citizen Service

Getting to work as a Policy intern at the London HQ of National Citizen Service (NCS) was a culmination of two years of active involvement with the organisation. It was, therefore, a pleasure to work on the project of getting the NCS Bill passed through the House of Commons (HoC), because it was through NCS that I had got to seize many opportunities to create good social change that were not available in my immediate vicinity. For the first time, I had a medium through which I could action my interest in politics, applying the lessons I had learnt in my sixth form classes to socio-political projects that sought to create a more united Britain.

My main role consisted of: drafting personalised briefings for MPs ahead of the HoC debate, after researching their policies using Hansard; collating, proofreading, and editing the data of NCS graduate experiences on Google Forms and Microsoft Excel; and editing written content on the NCS website. I also prepared arguments for possible topics that were likely to be mentioned in the House of Lords (HoL) Second Reading; for instance, 'disability/inclusion', and 'the transition of NCS to Royal Charter status'.

The NCS Leaders programme was another pivotal experience in my personal development, since it was designed to allow NCS graduates to further develop their leadership skills by taking part in activities like: debating at 10 Downing Street; working with Sky, and Dementia UK; partaking in the NCS national billboard campaign; and getting featured in a story for Leicester Mercury.

Some other experiences have included: raising 1,000 GBP for the RSPCA; holding an annual position on the Leicestershire Regional Youth Board; and getting our charity campaign aired on BBC News.
